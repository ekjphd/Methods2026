Week 8 — Chang

Autoethnography (Chang 2008)

  • Uses the researcher's own experience as data to illuminate cultural phenomena
  • The "auto" (self) + "ethno" (culture) + "graphy" (writing) — writing culture through self
  • Not memoir: the goal is analysis of the cultural/social, not self-expression
  • Requires the same rigor as other qualitative methods: thick description, reflexivity, theoretical framing
For T&T: being a digital researcher in digital environments is itself data — your experience of platforms, tools, and communities is analytically productive.
Week 8

Ethnography and Digital Ethnography

  • Sustained engagement with a community or setting — participant observation over time
  • Goal: thick description of cultural practices and meanings from the inside
  • Digital ethnography: online communities, platforms, digital practices
  • Challenge: where does "the field" begin and end online?
Week 8 — Rose

Discourse Analysis (Rose 2023)

  • Examines how language (and images, and interfaces) constructs reality, subjects, and power
  • Key questions: What does this text naturalize? What subject positions does it construct? What is excluded or made invisible?
  • Foucauldian discourse analysis: how does discourse produce knowledge and power?
  • Not just "what is said" but "what is made possible to say — and by whom"
Week 8 — Candy & Edmonds

Practice-Based Research (Candy & Edmonds 2018)

  • Research in which creative practice is central to the investigation
  • The artifact (game, installation, publication, website) can itself be a research contribution
  • Directly relevant to T&T students whose dissertations may include creative or design work
  • Requires: clear research question, documented process, written explication of the claim the artifact makes
Your creative work makes a methodological argument — what does building/making reveal that writing alone cannot?
