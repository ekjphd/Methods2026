Week 10

What Is Textual Analysis?

  • Examining a text — its structure, content, form, and social function
  • "Text" is broad: written documents, images, interfaces, search results, datasets
  • Asks: What does this text mean? How does it produce that meaning? What does it do?
  • Not summary or content description — analytical interpretation grounded in theory
Textual analysis is close reading with a theoretical lens — and a claim about what is at stake.
Week 10 — Rose

Rose's Framework for Textual Analysis

Compositional analysis: how is the text structured? What is foregrounded and backgrounded?
Content: what is present? Crucially, what is absent?
Social relations: what relationship does the text construct with its reader? Who is the implied subject?
Social production: what work does this text do in the world? What does it enable, normalize, or exclude?
Week 10 — Burdick et al.

Digital Humanities Case Studies

  • DH projects are themselves texts — they make arguments through design, data selection, visualization
  • What the DH case studies demonstrate: every interface embodies interpretive choices
  • Textual analysis applied to a DH project: what does this visualization argue? What data is excluded?
  • The medium is the method
Week 10 — Jones

Search Engine Discourse Analysis (Jones 2024)

  • Applies discourse analysis specifically to search result pages as texts
  • Search results construct a ranked, curated version of knowledge
  • What do the top results for a query naturalize? What worldview do they reproduce?
  • Extends Noble (Week 5): not just bias, but discourse — what knowledge regime does search enact?
