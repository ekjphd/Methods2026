Week 2

What Is an Experiment?

  • Researcher introduces a treatment (independent variable) and measures its effect on an outcome (dependent variable)
  • Random assignment separates experimental from control group
  • Goal: establish causal relationships, not just correlations
  • Pre/posttest design: measure before and after the intervention
Experiments are strong on internal validity but can struggle with external validity — results may not generalize beyond the lab.
Week 2

Pretest and Posttest Design

Pretest

  • Establishes baseline
  • Must match posttest construct
  • Can introduce sensitization (testing effect)

Posttest

  • Measures change after treatment
  • Compared against control group
  • Same instrument as pretest, or parallel form

Classic example: measure reading comprehension → apply annotation intervention → re-measure comprehension.

Week 2

Threats to Internal Validity

  • Maturation — participants change over time regardless of treatment
  • History — external events happen during the study
  • Testing effect — taking the pretest changes performance on the posttest
  • Selection bias — groups differ before treatment begins
  • Attrition — different kinds of people drop out across conditions
Week 2 — Ethics

Research Ethics: AoIR + Markham

  • AoIR 3.0: ethical treatment of human participants in internet research — informed consent, minimizing harm, privacy
  • Markham: "fabrication" as methodological response, not misconduct — protecting participants through anonymization, compositing
  • Ethics isn't just a compliance checklist — it's a design choice that shapes what counts as data
For T&T researchers: online participation (posts, profiles, DMs) raises ongoing questions about what is "public" data.
