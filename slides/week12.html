Week 12 — Rose

Visual Analysis (Rose 2023)

  • Three sites of meaning: production, image itself, audiencing
  • Three modalities: technological, compositional, social
  • Images are not transparent representations — they produce meaning through framing, cropping, color, sequence
  • Visual data requires different analytical tools than text — and different ethical considerations
Week 12 — Manovich

Cultural Analytics (Manovich 2020)

  • Using computational tools to analyze large cultural datasets — millions of images, texts, or artworks
  • Patterns invisible at human scale become visible at computational scale
  • The visualization is itself a form of argument — design choices are interpretive choices
  • Challenge: does scale produce insight, or just impressive images?
Week 12 — Underwood

A Genealogy of Distant Reading (Underwood 2017)

  • Traces the intellectual history of computational approaches to text analysis in literary studies
  • Distant reading: sacrificing depth for breadth — what patterns emerge across thousands of texts?
  • Underwood: distant reading makes claims differently than close reading — what does it mean to "find" a pattern in data?
  • Critical lens: which texts got digitized and are therefore in the dataset?
Week 12 — Tools

Tools for Projects as Data

TAGS — Twitter Archiving Google Sheet; harvest tweet data by hashtag, keyword, or account
Voyant Tools — browser-based text analysis: frequency, co-occurrence, trends across a corpus
AntConc — concordance and corpus analysis tool; excellent for close patterns in large text sets
NVivo — qualitative data analysis software; coding, query, and visualization

Access NVivo through UCF's Citrix virtual desktop environment.
