Week 1

Course Overview and Objectives

  • Twelve methods over twelve weeks — experimental, archival, qualitative, quantitative, computational, and more
  • Student-led presentations: you own your method
  • Instructor presentations: Dr. Johnson shares methods she actually uses
  • Lab activities: hands-on practice every week
  • Cumulative goal: your own methods short list by Week 11
This course is designed around the T&T PhD's six specializations — you'll encounter methods relevant to your specific path.
Week 1

T&T PhD Specializations

  • Digital Humanities
  • Digital Media
  • EPIC (Editing, Publishing, and Interdisciplinary Curating)
  • Public History
  • Rhetoric and Composition
  • Scientific and Technical Communication

The methods in this course are not siloed — they cross these boundaries constantly. Archival methods matter to DH and Public History alike; discourse analysis cuts across Rhetoric, STC, and Digital Media.

Week 1

How Class Works Each Week

  • Student presentation — one or two students teach a method to the seminar
  • Prepared Q&A — everyone comes with written questions
  • Dr. Johnson's Story — how the method shows up in real research
  • Small group lab — hands-on practice with the week's method
  • Report-out and debrief — what we noticed, what we'd do differently
