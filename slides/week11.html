A Synthesis Frame: Data Feminism

  • D'Ignazio & Klein: data science is not neutral — who counts, who gets counted, and who does the counting are political choices
  • Apply this lens across all the methods we've studied: whose knowledge does each method privilege? What gets excluded from each framework?
  • Methods are not tools — they are arguments about what counts as evidence

Week 11 — IRB Workshop

IRB Overview

  • IRB (Institutional Review Board) reviews research involving human participants
  • "Exempt" ≠ no review — it means exempt from certain federal regulations; IRB still determines this
  • Three review levels: Exempt, Expedited, Full Board
You cannot determine your own exemption — IRB does that.
Week 11 — IRB

IRB Key Concepts

Anonymous

No one — not even the researcher — can identify who participated.

Confidential

The researcher knows identities but will not disclose them.

Privacy

A person's desire to control access to information about themselves — relevant even for public data.

Week 11 — IRB

IRB Protocol Components

  • Protocol — full description of the study from start to end
  • Recruitment materials — how will you find participants?
  • Consent form — what do participants agree to?
  • Survey/questionnaire — all instruments must be reviewed
  • Data analysis plan — how will data be stored and protected?
  • Technology explanation — if software/platforms are involved

One dissertation study = potentially 10+ documents submitted to IRB.

Week 11 — Lab

Today's Lab: Methods Selection Activity

Each group receives a one-paragraph research scenario. Produce: (1) which method(s) you'd use and why, (2) likely IRB level and main ethical exposure, (3) a 3-sentence methods paragraph in the register of a proposal.

Debrief: groups read methods paragraphs aloud. Push back on hedging or vagueness.
