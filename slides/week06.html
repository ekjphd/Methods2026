Week 6

Why Use a Survey?

  • Fast, cheap, scalable — reach many participants
  • Captures self-reported opinions, attitudes, behaviors
  • Standardized format allows comparison across respondents
  • Weakness: what people say ≠ what they do; self-report bias; construct validity
Key question: can the thing I want to know actually be measured by asking people directly?
Week 6

Survey Question Types

Closed Questions

  • Likert scale items (agree–disagree, 1–5)
  • Multiple choice
  • Ranking
  • Semantic differential

Open Questions

  • Text box responses
  • Good for demographics and elaboration
  • Require coding to analyze
Week 6 — Common Mistakes

Question Pitfalls

Leading: "How great is this class?" — implies a correct answer
Double-barreled: "Is this course useful and enjoyable?" — two questions
Loaded: "When did you stop skipping class?" — assumes prior behavior
Vague: "Do you use technology?" — what counts as technology?
Double negative: "Is it impossible that the moon landing didn't happen?"
Rule: if a first-year student could misread it, rewrite it.
Week 6 — Exemplar

Rouse & Stanfill (2025): Quantifying Fandom

  • Survey-based study of AO3 users' identity, romance preferences, and platform habits
  • Demonstrates how survey methods can study fan communities and digital participation
  • Operationalizes "fandom" as a measurable construct
  • What can a survey tell us about AO3 that platform analysis can't? What can it not tell us?
