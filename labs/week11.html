Group Activity: Methods Selection Scenarios

Scenario Prompts

[Dr. Johnson prepares 3–4 one-paragraph research scenarios before class, using topics plausible for this cohort — one scenario per group. Scenarios should involve realistic T&T dissertation contexts: e.g., a study of Discord communities in a specific fandom; a historical analysis of a digitized newspaper archive; a project measuring learning outcomes from a DH tool; an autoethnographic project about platform labor.]

For Each Scenario, Your Group Produces:

  1. Method selection: Which method or combination of methods from the semester would you use? Why these rather than others?
  2. IRB analysis: What level of IRB review would this likely require (exempt, expedited, or full board)? What is the main ethical exposure?
  3. Methods paragraph: A 3-sentence methods paragraph written in the register of an actual proposal — not a description of what you'd do, but how you'd write about it.
Debrief: Groups read methods paragraphs aloud. Push back on hedging or vagueness. Does the paragraph actually commit to anything?
IRB Quick Reference
  • Exempt — minimal risk; no vulnerable populations; typically surveys, interviews, archival research
  • Expedited — greater than minimal risk but no full board review required; adult participants in non-sensitive topics
  • Full Board — vulnerable populations (minors, prisoners, people with disabilities); deception; sensitive topics
  • Key reminder: You cannot determine your own exemption — IRB makes that determination
