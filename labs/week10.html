Group Activity: Apply Rose's Framework

Shared Text

[Dr. Johnson selects before class — one of: a search results page screenshot for a contested query, a Wikipedia article on a contested topic, or a section of a DH project's interface. Same text for all groups.]

Analysis Questions (Rose's Framework)

  1. Compositional structure: How is the text organized? What does the design foreground? What is minimized?
  2. Present and absent content: What is included? What is noticeably absent?
  3. Social relations: What relationship does this text construct with its reader? Who is the implied subject?
  4. Social production: What work does this text do in the world? What does it enable, normalize, or exclude?

Final 8 Minutes: Write an Analytical Paragraph

Each group writes one paragraph of textual analysis in the register of a published academic article. Not bullet points — a paragraph that makes a claim and supports it.

Groups read paragraphs aloud in debrief. Does it make an argument? Does it hedge too much? Is it just description?
