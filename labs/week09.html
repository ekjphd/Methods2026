Group Activity: Three-Stage Coding

Shared Dataset

[Dr. Johnson prepares before class: 8–10 social media posts, Reddit comments, or short interview excerpts on a technology topic. Same dataset for all groups.]

Stage 1: Open Coding (12 min)

Generate codes for every meaningful passage. Rules:

  • No preconceived categories — stay close to the language of the text
  • Every group member codes independently for the first 5 minutes, then compare
  • A code is an interpretation, not just a label

Stage 2: Axial Coding

Identify clusters and relationships between your codes. What categories emerge? What causes or enables what?

Stage 3: Write Your Theory

Write one sentence stating the provisional theory your codes point toward. This sentence should make a claim — not just describe patterns.

Debrief: Compare theory sentences across groups. Same data — different theories. What explains the difference?
Materials
  • Qualitative dataset — [Dr. Johnson to prepare: 8–10 social media posts or interview excerpts on a technology topic, printed or shared digitally]
