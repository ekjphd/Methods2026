Group Activity: Two Frameworks, One Dataset

Shared Material

All groups work from the same public social media account or thread. [Dr. Johnson selects 10–15 posts before class — one account or thread, publicly accessible.]

Half the groups apply Brock's CTDA framework; half apply Carah & Dobson's algorithmic hotness lens.

CTDA Groups (Brock)

  • What does the platform's design afford or constrain?
  • How does race or power operate in the interface and content?
  • What subject positions does the platform make available?

Algorithmic Hotness Groups (Carah & Dobson)

  • How is the content optimized for platform visibility?
  • What body or identity norms does that optimization reproduce?
  • What patterns in engagement metrics reflect platform logic rather than community preference?

Debrief

Pair one CTDA group with one algorithmic hotness group to compare findings on the same posts. Where did the frameworks converge? Where did they diverge — and why?

Materials
  • Social media posts for analysis — [Dr. Johnson to select and share before class: 10–15 posts from a public account or thread]
  • Brock (2018) — Critical technocultural discourse analysis [UCF library]
  • Carah & Dobson (2016) — Algorithmic hotness [open access]
  • Fiesler & Proferes (2018) — Participant perceptions of Twitter research ethics [open access]
